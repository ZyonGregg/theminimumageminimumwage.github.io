The Issue

Every day, millions of children are forced into work that endangers their health, robs them of education, and traps them in cycles of poverty. This page is dedicated to understanding the roots of child labor, the conditions that sustain it, and how we can dismantle the systems that exploit childhood for profit.

What is Child Labor?
Child labour refers to the exploitation of children through any form of work that deprives them of their childhood, interferes with their ability to build education, and maintain their physical and mental welfare. And since 2020, approximately 160 million children, nearly 1 in 10 globally were engaged in child labour. About 79 million of them were involved in hazardous work, including forced labour, trafficking, sexual exploitation, and work in harmful environments.

What Can Be Done?
Ending child labor requires global collaboration. We must enforce laws, support education, raise awareness, and ensure ethical business practices.
