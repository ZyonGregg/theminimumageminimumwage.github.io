Improvements and Progress

Scroll to explore how we're making a difference.

1

2020 – ILO & UNICEF Joint Report

The world witnessed a significant reduction of approximately 94 million child laborers between 2000 and 2016 — a testament to global advocacy and policy reform. However, this momentum began to slow in subsequent years, with progress stalling in many regions. The 2020 ILO and UNICEF joint report emphasized the urgent need for renewed international cooperation, stronger enforcement of labor laws, and targeted interventions to protect vulnerable children from exploitation.

2

2021 – Global March Against Child Labour

In 2021, non-governmental organizations and youth-led movements from over 140 countries joined forces in one of the most widespread campaigns against child labor in recent history. Their efforts spanned continents and cultures, harnessing the power of both on-the-ground activism and digital platforms to raise global consciousness. Through awareness drives, virtual conferences, school programs, and social media outreach, they were able to engage and educate more than 10 million individuals. These initiatives not only provided critical information and resources but also inspired collective responsibility—empowering young people, families, educators, and policymakers to take meaningful action in the fight to protect children’s rights and end exploitation.

3

2023 – Legislation in Action

In 2023, several countries across Latin America, Southeast Asia, and Europe introduced new labor laws aimed at strengthening the fight against child labor. These legislative reforms prioritized routine inspections, stricter enforcement protocols, and increased penalties for non-compliant industries. Governments worked alongside civil society and international organizations to ensure these laws were not only passed but actively implemented. By holding industries more accountable and improving transparency within supply chains, these efforts marked a critical step forward in safeguarding children’s rights and preventing exploitation in vulnerable communities.

4

2024 – Youth Advocacy Rises

In 2024, youth-driven movements like Minimum Age, Minimum Age began harnessing the power of technology and storytelling to spark change on a global scale. By combining social media campaigns, interactive platforms, digital art, and personal narratives, these initiatives created compelling content that resonated deeply with younger audiences. Thousands of students across different countries became informed, engaged, and empowered to advocate for children’s rights. Through online forums, webinars, and creative collaborations, the movement not only raised awareness about the realities of child labor but also built a sense of solidarity among youth determined to make a difference in their communities and beyond.

5

The Next Steps

  • Stronger enforcement of child labor laws
  • Universal access to primary and secondary education
  • Global supply chain transparency
  • Empowerment programs for children and families
