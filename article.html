The Hidden Cost of Childhood

by Advocacy Team · Updated April 2024

In the corners of factories, on harsh fields, and in cramped homes, children as young as five are being denied their right to a childhood. Child labor is not just a violation of human rights — it is a theft of innocence, education, and potential.

According to the International Labour Organization, over 160 million children worldwide are involved in child labor. These children are often subjected to hazardous environments, exploitation, and physical and psychological harm.

Many of the goods we consume — from chocolate and coffee to fast fashion and electronics — are made with the hidden hands of child labor. It's a sobering realization that the global supply chain is deeply entangled in this injustice.

“Children should never work in fields, but on dreams.” — Kailash Satyarthi

There is hope. With proper education, support, and legislation, we can pull children out of labor and place them into classrooms. Advocacy groups, youth organizations, and conscious consumers have the power to break this cycle.

By choosing ethically sourced products, supporting organizations combating child labor, and spreading awareness, we become part of a global solution. Let’s demand better — not just for our future, but for theirs.
